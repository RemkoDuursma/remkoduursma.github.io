About

Remko Duursma is a Senior Lecturer (80% research, “Assistant Professor”) at the Hawkesbury Institute for the Environment, a research institute embedded within Western Sydney University.

My research revolves around understanding vegetation and function with models and data. I develop models of plant ecophysiological processes such as plant water use, stomatal conductance and photosynthesis, canopy architecture and light interception, biomass allocation and growth. This work is done with many collaborators.

Besides developing and applying models, I have a strong focus on data synthesis and analysis. As a result of various data analysis projects, I aim to share code in the hope that it may be useful to others.
